# **Playoff Picture Intensifies: Bubble Teams Fight for Final Spots as NBA Season Nears Climax**

> **Grounding Score: 100% — Verified**
> Desk: Sports | Published: Mon Apr 06 2026



---

As the 2025-26 NBA regular season careens towards its April 12 conclusion, the playoff landscape is largely set at the top, but a furious battle for the remaining direct berths and crucial Play-In Tournament seeding is dominating headlines in both conferences. While perennial contenders have punched their tickets, several teams are locked in a high-stakes race over the final week, where every possession and every outcome holds immense weight. The Play-In Tournament is slated for April 14-17, leading into the official start of the playoffs on April 18.

In the Eastern Conference, the Detroit Pistons have firmly secured the No. 1 seed with an impressive 57-21 record, a testament to a season that has defied many expectations. They are followed by the Boston Celtics (53-25), New York Knicks (50-28), and Cleveland Cavaliers (49-29), all of whom have also clinched outright playoff spots. The real drama unfolds from the fifth seed downwards, where the Atlanta Hawks (45-33) currently hold the fifth position, enjoying a slight cushion over the Philadelphia 76ers (43-35) and Toronto Raptors (43-35), who are tied for the sixth and final direct playoff spot. Just behind them, the Charlotte Hornets (43-36), Orlando Magic (42-36), and Miami Heat (41-37) are embroiled in a desperate fight to solidify their Play-In Tournament positions. The Milwaukee Bucks, Chicago Bulls, Indiana Pacers, Brooklyn Nets, and Washington Wizards have unfortunately been eliminated from postseason contention. Notably, last season's Eastern Conference champions, the Indiana Pacers, will miss the playoffs this year.

Out West, the Oklahoma City Thunder have asserted their dominance, clinching the No. 1 seed with an outstanding 62-16 record. The San Antonio Spurs (59-19) have also clinched a playoff berth, marking their first return to the postseason since 2019, largely thanks to the phenomenal play of Victor Wembanyama. The Los Angeles Lakers (50-28), Denver Nuggets (50-28), and Houston Rockets (48-29) have also secured playoff berths and are tightly contested in the 3-5 seeding race. The Minnesota Timberwolves (46-32) are in a strong position for the sixth seed but have not yet clinched a direct playoff spot. The Play-In Tournament field in the West is confirmed, featuring the Phoenix Suns (43-35) in the seventh spot, battling for home-court advantage against the Portland Trail Blazers (40-38) and Los Angeles Clippers (40-38). The Golden State Warriors (36-42) are locked into the tenth spot. The Memphis Grizzlies, New Orleans Pelicans, Dallas Mavericks, Utah Jazz, and Sacramento Kings have been eliminated.

The intensity of these final games is amplified by critical head-to-head matchups. In the Eastern Conference, teams like the Raptors, 76ers, Hornets, Magic, and Heat will face off in games that could swing their playoff fortunes dramatically. In the West, the battle between the Lakers, Nuggets, and Rockets for seeding within the top six is poised to go down to the wire. The upcoming clash between the Portland Trail Blazers and the LA Clippers, for instance, could determine who gains a crucial home-court advantage in the play-in tournament.

Individual brilliance continues to shape the playoff picture. Oklahoma City's Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is having an MVP-caliber season, leading the charge for the top-seeded Thunder. Jayson Tatum's consistent scoring keeps the Boston Celtics among the East's elite. While his team is eliminated, Luka Doncic of the Dallas Mavericks remains the league's leading scorer at 33.5 points per game. Nikola Jokic continues to anchor the Denver Nuggets with his all-around dominance, averaging 13.0 rebounds and 10.8 assists per game. Even at 41, LeBron James of the Los Angeles Lakers is still contributing significantly, as evidenced by his 30-point performance against the Mavericks, though the Lakers ultimately fell. Cade Cunningham has been instrumental in the Detroit Pistons' surprising rise to the top seed in the East.

With only days remaining until the regular season concludes on April 12, the stakes couldn't be higher. The Play-In Tournament, set for April 14-17, will determine the final two playoff spots in each conference, adding another layer of thrilling, single-elimination drama. Teams will not only be fighting for a playoff berth but also for favorable seeding to avoid formidable opponents in the initial rounds. The next few days promise to deliver high-octane basketball as teams jockey for position, setting the stage for what is expected to be a captivating 2026 NBA Playoffs.
