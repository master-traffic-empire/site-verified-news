# Bitcoin and Crypto Markets Roar Back in April 2026 Amid Tariff Jitters, Strong ETF Inflows

> **Grounding Score: 100% — Verified**
> Desk: Finance | Published: Tue Apr 07 2026

_Crypto assets jump as Bitcoin ETFs record significant inflows, offsetting earlier tariff-induced fears and signaling shifting institutional sentiment._


---

NEW YORK – Bitcoin and the broader cryptocurrency markets experienced a notable surge on Monday, April 6, 2026, with Bitcoin's price reclaiming the crucial $70,000 mark. The CoinDesk Bitcoin Price Index rose by 3.59% to close at $69,755.09, reaching an intraday high of $70,317.85. This rally was largely propelled by a significant rebound in Bitcoin spot Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF) inflows and a tentative easing of geopolitical tensions, even as uncertainty surrounding new Trump administration tariffs continues to cast a shadow over global trade.

The positive momentum extended across the digital asset landscape, with the total cryptocurrency market capitalization adding over $60 billion in a single day, pushing it to approximately $2.45 trillion. Ethereum, the second-largest cryptocurrency, also saw a robust performance, increasing by 3.56% to reach $2,133.07. This widespread upturn suggests a renewed investor appetite for digital assets, contrasting sharply with the prevailing "Extreme Fear" sentiment reflected by a Crypto Fear & Greed Index reading of 13 on the same day.

The rally occurred amidst a backdrop of ongoing economic uncertainty, particularly concerning the Trump administration's evolving tariff policies. Earlier in February 2026, the U.S. Supreme Court ruled against the legality of broad tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act (IEEPA). In response, President Donald Trump swiftly invoked Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974, implementing a 10% global tariff on most imports, with discussions of potentially raising it to 15%. This "Liberation Day" tariff package, which took effect on April 5, had initially caused Bitcoin to dip to $66,500 and contributed to a prolonged period of "extreme fear" in the crypto markets. However, Monday's surge suggested that some investors might be viewing cryptocurrencies as a hedge against traditional market volatility fueled by trade policy unpredictability, or reacting to reported easing of Mideast tensions.

A primary catalyst for Monday's market upturn was the substantial inflow into Bitcoin spot ETFs. These funds collectively attracted $471.31 million in net inflows on April 6, marking their best single-day performance in at least six weeks. BlackRock's IBIT fund led the charge with $181.88 million in inflows, followed by Fidelity's FBTC with $147.32 million, and Ark's ARKB with $118.76 million. This impressive return of institutional capital signals a significant shift in sentiment, particularly after a period of outflows in early April. Ethereum spot ETFs also recorded a combined net inflow of $120 million, indicating broader institutional interest across the crypto spectrum.

Despite the day's strong performance, some analysts maintain a cautious outlook. Bitfinex analysts highlighted that recent price increases have been primarily driven by capital flows rather than improving underlying fundamentals, warning of potential market fragility. However, major players continue to demonstrate conviction in the long-term value of Bitcoin. On April 6, Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy), a prominent corporate holder of Bitcoin, announced the acquisition of an additional 4,871 bitcoins for approximately $329.9 million. This move further reinforces institutional demand and conviction in the asset, despite its current average cost basis for Strategy being $75,644 per coin, above current market prices.

Looking ahead, the interplay between evolving trade policies, global economic stability, and the growing institutional adoption of cryptocurrencies will continue to shape market dynamics. While the immediate surge reflects renewed investor confidence, the market remains sensitive to geopolitical developments and future tariff pronouncements from the Trump administration. Experts are divided on Bitcoin's trajectory for the remainder of 2026, with some predicting a potential rise to $80,000-$100,000 if macro conditions improve, and others, like Bernstein analysts, reiterating a $150,000 price target for the year. The ability of Bitcoin to sustain its position above key resistance levels and attract consistent ETF inflows will be crucial in navigating the persistent macroeconomic uncertainties.
