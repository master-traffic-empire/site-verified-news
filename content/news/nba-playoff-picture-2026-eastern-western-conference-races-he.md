NBA Playoff Picture 2026: Eastern, Western Conference Races Heat Up in April
Final Week Fury: East Play-In Picture Murky, West Locks in Top Seeds as Regular Season Nears End.

The 2025-2026 NBA regular season is hurtling toward its dramatic conclusion, with the final day of games set for Sunday, April 12. As of Tuesday, April 7, the playoff landscape features a mix of established contenders and a chaotic scramble for the remaining play-in tournament spots, particularly in the Eastern Conference. While several teams have already punched their tickets to the postseason, the fight for seeding and home-court advantage is reaching a fever pitch across both conferences, promising a thrilling end to the campaign before the SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament tips off on April 14.

In the Eastern Conference, the Detroit Pistons (57-21) have impressively clinched the top seed, locking up home-court advantage throughout the conference playoffs. The Boston Celtics (52-25) and New York Knicks (50-28) have also secured their playoff berths, currently holding the second and third seeds, respectively. The Cleveland Cavaliers (48-29) are firmly in fourth. The real battle intensifies from the fifth seed downwards, with the Atlanta Hawks (45-33) and Philadelphia 76ers (43-34) currently holding the fifth and sixth spots, respectively, aiming to avoid the perilous Play-In Tournament. The Toronto Raptors (43-34) are tied with Philadelphia but sit seventh due to tiebreakers, making their final games crucial to potentially leapfrog into a guaranteed playoff berth. Just behind them, the Charlotte Hornets (42-36) at eighth, and the Miami Heat (41-37) at tenth, are embroiled in a tight race with the Orlando Magic (41-36), who are currently ninth, for play-in positioning. Two of these teams are guaranteed to miss the postseason entirely.

Out West, the top of the standings offers more clarity, yet crucial seeding remains fluid. The Oklahoma City Thunder (62-16) have officially clinched the No. 1 seed and the best record in the conference. The San Antonio Spurs (59-19) have secured the second seed after a strong season led by Victor Wembanyama, making their first playoff appearance since 2019. The Los Angeles Lakers and Denver Nuggets are in a deadlock at 50-28, battling for the third and fourth seeds, with the Nuggets riding an eight-game winning streak. The Houston Rockets (49-29) have secured the fifth seed, followed by the Minnesota Timberwolves (46-31) in sixth, who are still looking to officially clinch to avoid the play-in. The Phoenix Suns (43-35) are currently locked into the seventh seed and will participate in the Play-In Tournament, a position they hoped to avoid due to struggles with consistency. The Portland Trail Blazers (40-38) are in the eighth spot, with the Los Angeles Clippers (39-38) and Golden State Warriors (36-41) rounding out the top ten, all fighting for their Play-In lives.

Key matchups this week will significantly shape the final standings. Tonight, Tuesday, April 7, the Minnesota Timberwolves can clinch a top-six playoff spot with a win over the Indiana Pacers combined with a Phoenix Suns loss to the Houston Rockets. In the Eastern Conference, the Miami Heat's crucial two-game series against the Toronto Raptors could determine their Play-In fate, with a loss potentially locking them into the Play-In Tournament. Another pivotal East contest features the Boston Celtics against the New York Knicks, a potential first-round preview with significant seeding implications. The regular season concludes on April 12, with all 30 teams in action.

As teams jostle for position, the stakes couldn't be higher. The Play-In Tournament, scheduled for April 14-17, will determine the final two playoff spots in each conference, featuring the 7th through 10th seeds in a high-stakes mini-tournament. The winners of the 7-8 seed games will claim the seventh seed, while the losers will face the winners of the 9-10 seed elimination games for the eighth seed. The 2026 NBA Playoffs will officially commence on April 18, with the NBA Finals projected to begin on June 3. The road to the Larry O'Brien Trophy is set to be a grueling test of endurance and skill, with every game in the coming week holding immense weight for teams aiming to extend their seasons.
